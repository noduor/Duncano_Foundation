Our Programs

Comprehensive education and empowerment initiatives transforming lives across Africa

Duncano Education Scholarship Grant (DESG) Program

Empowering African children and youth through quality education

The DESG Program empowers African children and youth by providing access to quality education, aiming to reduce inequalities and promote social and economic growth across communities. Our goal is to equip learners with the skills, knowledge, and opportunities needed to build successful futures and contribute meaningfully to their societies.

Key Initiatives

Post-Secondary Scholarships

Supporting learners pursuing certificates, diplomas, and undergraduate studies, with priority given to technical and vocational education (TVET) and STEM courses that drive Africa's development.

Technical & Vocational Education

Preparing students for future job markets with a focus on practical and technical skills through TVET programs.

Bursary Programs

Providing financial support to diversify student populations, including emergency bursaries for learners facing unexpected hardships.

Master's Scholarships

Offering advanced education opportunities to exceptional students with potential to create significant societal impact.

Impact: Together, these initiatives foster educational empowerment, enhance skills development, and create pathways for sustainable community advancement.

Duncano HELB Relief (DHR) Program

Empowering Kenyans by resolving critical HELB loan obligations

The DHR Program empowers Kenyans by resolving critical HELB loan obligations, enabling access to essential documents, CRB clearance, employment opportunities, and business financing. Our goal is to remove financial barriers that limit both economic participation and educational advancement.

Key Initiatives

HELB Loan Settlement

Providing full or partial repayment of outstanding HELB loans to support economic empowerment and improve financial freedom for defaulters.

Student Funding Support

Bridging financial gaps for active university, college, and TVET students who receive insufficient HELB support, ensuring full tuition coverage and uninterrupted education.

Impact: Together, these initiatives enhance access to education and economic opportunities, fostering financial inclusion and empowerment.

Duncano Study and Work (DSW) Program

Empowering Kenyan youth by providing opportunities to earn while they learn.

The DSW Program empowers Kenyan youth by providing opportunities to earn while they learn, fostering independence, reducing financial dependency, and equipping students with practical work experience. In partnership with universities, TVET institutions, and private sector organizations, the program sponsors initiatives that allow students to engage in part-timestrong> work during school days, through campus-based programs, local partner placements, or online employment opportunities. This structure enables students to generate income to support tuition, housing, daily living expenses, and family needs, while simultaneously developing professional skills and contributing to the national economy.

Key Initiatives

Campus Work-Study Grants

Providing full or partial repayment of outstanding HELB loans to support economic empowerment and improve financial freedom for defaulters.

Campus Work-Study Grants

Funding for programs within universities and TVETs that create part-time employment opportunities aligned with students’ academic schedules.

Partner Placement Opportunitie

Collaborations with companies and organizations to offer students work placements close to their institutions for practical experience.

Remote and Online Work Program

Supporting students in accessing flexible online employment opportunities that complement their studies.

Financial Literacy & Career Development Workshop

Training students on managing income, budgeting, and preparing for professional careers.

Student Entrepreneurial Support

Business Grants and mentorship for students with top and brilliant businesses ideas or income-generating initiatives alongside their studies.

Family Support Assistance

Structured programs to ensure students can provide financial support to families while maintaining academic performance.

Impact: Through these initiatives, the DSW Program cultivates a financially independent, skilled, and adaptable student population, fostering a student-friendly economy where education and income generation go hand in hand, ultimately contributing to national economic growth and social development.

Duncano Continuing Education Fund Scholarships (DCEFS) Program

Supporting individuals who wish to continue their education after interruptions

The DCEFS Program provides targeted scholarships to support individuals who wish to continue their education after interruptions, with a special focus on school dropouts and young mothers. By removing financial and social barriers, the program empowers learners to reclaim their educational journeys and build brighter futures for themselves, their families, and their communities.

Key Initiatives

Scholarships for School Dropouts

Financial support for learners who left school due to personal, social, or economic challenges, enabling them to resume formal education.

Support for Young Mothers

Scholarships and mentorship for mothers who wish to reenter the school system after leaving due to early pregnancies, empowering them to complete their education and pursue career opportunities.

Rescue from Early Marriages

Initiatives aimed at reintegrating girls who were forced into early marriages back into the education system, ensuring they regain access to learning and life-changing opportunities.

Holistic Support Services

Complementary support such as counseling, mentorship, and community engagement to ensure students can successfully reintegrate and thrive in school.

Community Advocacy

Programs to raise awareness about the importance of education for girls and young women, working with families and communities to prevent early school dropout and early marriages.

Impact: Through these initiatives, the DCEFS Program fosters educational inclusion, promotes equality, and creates pathways for young women and other marginalized learners to achieve their full potential.

Duncano School Infrastructure Development (DSID) Program

Improving physical infrastructure of schools in under-resourced areas

The DSID Program provides grants and sponsorships to improve the physical infrastructure of schools, particularly in under-resourced and remote areas. By creating safe, modern, and eco-friendly learning environments, the program enhances educational outcomes and fosters holistic development for students.

Key Initiatives

Classroom and Laboratory Construction

Building new classrooms and fully equipped science and technology labs to replace dilapidated structures or open-air learning spaces.

Eco-Friendly School Designs

Promoting sustainable construction practices using environmentally friendly materials and energy-efficient technologies.

Clean Water and Sanitation Facilities

Installing water supply systems and sanitation facilities in schools that lack access to clean water, supporting health and hygiene.

Critical Infrastructure Support

Providing other essential infrastructure such as libraries, computer centers, and multipurpose halls to create conducive learning environments.

Upgrading Under-Resourced Schools

Transforming schools with minimal or no facilities into modern, fully functional institutions, ensuring all students have access to quality learning spaces.

Impact: Through these initiatives, the DSID Program strengthens the educational ecosystem, improves student engagement and performance, and promotes sustainable and inclusive school development in communities across Africa.
